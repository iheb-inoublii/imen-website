Imen's Soundtrack

💃

The Four Moods of Imen 🎚️

Her playlist doesn't have genres. It has personalities.

🪕

El Basta Mode

Mezoued, bendir and darbouka — the pure Tunisian basta vibe of Hedi Habbouba, Salah Farzit and Samir Loussif. The moment the mezoued starts, Imen's shoulders sign a contract with the rhythm. No refunds.

🎻

Tunisian Classics Mode

Hedi Jouini under the jasmine ("Taht El Yasmina Fel Lil"), Saliha's golden voice, Oulaya, Ali Riahi... The old recordings crackle a little, and that crackle is Imen's favorite instrument.

🌧️

Sad Songs Mode

Rain on the window, tea in hand, the saddest playlist known to science. Imen isn't sad — she's directing an emotional film in her head, and the soundtrack must be PERFECT.

🇮🇳

Bollywood Mode

Arijit Singh for the feelings, A.R. Rahman for the goosebumps, Lata Mangeshkar for the classics. One song in, the living room becomes a film set and Imen is the entire cast.

The Indian Dance Department 💃

Self-taught, fully committed, surprisingly good.

🪩

The Trigger

Any Bollywood song, anywhere. A wedding, a kitchen, a serious moment — it does not matter. The beat drops, Imen rises.

🖐️

The Technique

The hand waves, the head slide, the dramatic spin — practiced in front of a mirror that has seen more choreography than most Bollywood directors.

👀

The Audience

House plants (supportive), family (used to it), neighbors (concerned at first, fans now). Her students would lose their minds if they knew. One day, kids. One day.
