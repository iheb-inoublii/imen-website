Nabeul, Habibet El Cap Bon 🍊

Postcards from Home 💌

Nabeul, the pearl of Cap Bon — Imen's hometown and the source of all her superpowers.

🏺

The Pottery

Nabeul's ceramics are famous across Tunisia — hand-painted plates, jars, and tiles. The giant pottery jar at the city entrance? That's not decoration, that's a monument to craftsmanship. Imen grew up surrounded by art.

🍊

The Oranges

Every spring, Nabeul smells like orange blossom. Families distill zhar (orange-blossom water) the old way, and the whole city becomes a perfume. Other cities have seasons; Nabeul has fragrances.

🌶️

The Harissa

Cap Bon peppers, sun-dried and crushed into the world's greatest condiment. In Nabeul, harissa is not a sauce — it's a family member. It has a seat at the table.

🏖️

The Sea

Long sandy beaches where summer lasts forever and watermelon tastes better with sea salt in the air. Imen's childhood soundtrack: waves, seagulls, and someone calling everyone to eat.

A Nabeul Menu, Imen-Approved 🍽️

🥘

Slata Mechouia

Grilled peppers and tomatoes, crushed with garlic and olive oil. Looks innocent. Is not innocent. Imen adds extra peppers "for flavor." Sure, Imen. For flavor.

🍲

Couscous with Hout

Fish couscous, Cap Bon style — generous, fragrant, and spicy enough to make you see your ancestors. They wave. They're proud.

🥖

Fricassé

The little fried sandwich with tuna, harissa, olives and egg. Imen's rule: if your eyes don't water a little, it's basically a dessert.

🍮

Anything with Zhar

Orange-blossom water in the coffee, in the desserts, in the air. The only Nabeul specialty that doesn't burn — it just smells like heaven.
