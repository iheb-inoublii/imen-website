The Beauty of Old Tunisia 🏺

The Old Wardrobe Imen Dreams Of 👗

Traditional Tunisian clothes — the originals that make every modern outfit jealous.

👰

The Keswa

The traditional bridal outfit — velvet, gold thread, and embroidery so detailed it takes months to make. In Nabeul weddings, when the bride appears in her keswa, even the music holds its breath.

🤍

The Sefseri

The silky white veil Tunisian grandmothers wore like a cloud wrapped around them. Elegant, mysterious, and somehow they could carry groceries, gossip, and grandchildren all while keeping it perfectly in place.

🧵

The Fouta w Blouza

The classic two-piece with woven stripes and a fitted top — worn for centuries along the coast. Old photos of women in fouta w blouza look like paintings. Imen agrees: they ARE paintings.

🔴

The Chechia

The famous red felt cap, hand-made through seven different crafts. Once the pride of an entire souk in Tunis (Souk Ech-Chaouachine). One hat, seven artisans, infinite style.

✨

The Jebba

Flowing, embroidered, worn for Eid and weddings. A jebba doesn't have a size — it has a presence. When grandpa entered in his jebba, the room rearranged itself.

🧿

The Jewelry

Khomsa pendants, coral beads from the north, silver from Djerba. Old Tunisian jewelry wasn't an accessory — it was the family savings account, but make it fashion.

A Walk Through Time ⏳

From Carthage to grandma's couscous — scroll and time-travel.

814 BC — Carthage is founded

Queen Dido founds a city so great that Rome got jealous. The Mediterranean's first main character moment — Tunisian, of course.

670 — Kairouan rises

One of the oldest cities of the Islamic world, with a Great Mosque whose courtyard collects rain like it's harvesting the sky.

1100s — The Medina of Tunis bustles

Souks of perfume, copper, fabric and chechias. You enter to buy one thing, leave four hours later with rugs, sweets, and three new lifelong friends.

1920s — The age of azure

Sidi Bou Said is painted white and blue forever. Possibly history's greatest aesthetic decision — old Tunisia understood beauty before it was trendy.

Every Sunday since — Grandma's kitchen

Couscous that could resolve international conflicts, olive oil poured with confidence, and the famous words: "You're not leaving until you eat more."

"Old Tunisia didn't have filters. It didn't need them. The sefseri was white, the doors were blue, the jasmine was real."

Old Tunisian Wisdom 🍵

🫖

On Tea

Tunisian mint tea must be poured from a dramatic height. The higher the pour, the deeper the love. Grandmas pour from orbit.

🧿

On Luck

The blue eye and the khomsa protect the house. Old Tunisian home security: zero cameras, one hundred percent coverage.

🌼

On Jasmine

Machmoum behind the ear — the original Tunisian notification that someone nice is nearby. In Nabeul, the orange blossom adds a second perfume layer. Imen's hometown advantage.
