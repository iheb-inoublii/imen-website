Old Tunisian Times 🏺

The Blue Doors of Sidi Bou Said

A whole village painted white and blue. Coincidence? Imen says no. Imen says destiny.

🚪

The Doors

Every door in Sidi Bou Said is blue, studded with black nail patterns. Legend says if a door is not blue, it's just shy and will come around eventually.

☕

Café des Délices

Mint tea with pine nuts, overlooking the sea. The tea is hot, the view is blue, and the tourists are taking 600 photos. Imen took 601.

🌼

The Jasmine Sellers

Machmoum — little jasmine bouquets worn behind the ear. The original Tunisian notification: "someone nice is nearby."

🪟

The Moucharabieh

Carved blue window cages where grandmas observed the whole street. The original surveillance system — no battery, 100% accurate, runs on curiosity.

A Walk Through Time ⏳

From Carthage to grandma's couscous — scroll and time-travel.

814 BC — Carthage is founded

Queen Dido founds a city so great that Rome got jealous. The Mediterranean's first main character moment — Tunisian, of course.

670 — Kairouan rises

One of the oldest cities of the Islamic world, with a Great Mosque whose courtyard collects rain like it's harvesting the sky. Blue sky. Imen approves.

1100s — The Medina of Tunis bustles

Souks of perfume, copper, and chechias. You enter to buy one thing, leave four hours later with rugs, sweets, and three new lifelong friends.

1920s — The age of azure

Baron d'Erlanger convinces Sidi Bou Said to keep everything white and blue, forever. Possibly history's greatest aesthetic decision. Imen would have signed that decree twice.

Every Sunday since — Grandma's kitchen

Couscous that could resolve international conflicts, olive oil poured with confidence, and the famous words: "You're not leaving until you eat more."

"They painted the whole town white and blue, hung jasmine in the air, and waited a few generations for Imen to show up and call it home."

Old Tunisian Wisdom 🍵

🫖

On Tea

Tunisian mint tea must be poured from a dramatic height. The higher the pour, the deeper the love. Grandmas pour from orbit.

🧿

On Luck

The blue eye (khomsa nearby for backup) protects the house. It is also — conveniently — blue. Everything in this culture was preparing for Imen.

🍉

On Summer

Old Tunisian summers: watermelon by the sea, a nap that lasts geological eras, and the sound of waves. No episodes were skipped, because there were no episodes. Dark times.
