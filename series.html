Imen's Golden Screen 🎬

The Hall of Fame — Hover to Flip 🃏

The Tunisian classics that own a permanent place in Imen's evenings.

🛋️

Choufli Hal

2005–2009 · The legend

Dr. Slimane Labyedh's clinic, Sbouii's catastrophes (the great Sofiène Chaâri 💙), Azza at the desk... Imen knows every episode, quotes every line, and still laughs like it's the first watch. "Chnowa el mochkol?!"

🏚️

Mnamet Aroussia

2000 · The masterpiece

Mouna Noureddine as Aroussia — the iron matriarch of an old family estate, twins fighting over inheritance, secrets in every room. Tunisian drama at its absolute peak. Imen watches it like a film critic: in silence, with tea.

🌙

Gamret Sidi Mahrous

The 90s classic

Old Tunis houses, family sagas, and dialogue your grandmother still quotes. The kind of series where the medina itself is a main character. Exactly Imen's aesthetic: old Tunisia, on screen.

🚪

El Khottab Al Bab

90s · The suitors saga

Aziza and the endless parade of suitors at the door — the series that taught a whole generation that "no" is a complete sentence. Old-school Ramadan TV, when the whole family watched together.

👵

Nsibti Laaziza

The chaos engine

The mother-in-law that broke the chaos scale. Every Tunisian family swears they have their own Daldoula. Imen watches and takes notes — strictly for science.

🇮🇳

The Indian Corner

Drama × 1000

When Tunisian series end, Bollywood begins: three-hour epics, plot twists, weddings, rain scenes, and at least one song on a mountain. Imen cries at minute 40. Every time. On schedule.

"Other people have comfort food. Imen has comfort episodes — and Sbouii has been ruining Dr. Slimane's clinic, comfortably, since 2005."

A Binge Night, Hour by Hour 🌙

20:00 — The plan

"One episode of Choufli Hal and I'll work on my thesis." Narrator: the thesis remained untouched.

22:30 — The bargaining

"Episodes are basically 40 minutes, which is basically 30, which is basically nothing." Flawless researcher logic from a future Master of Computer Science.

01:00 — The crossover

Tunisian series finished, a Bollywood film starts "just to see the first song." The first song is at minute 25. The film is 3 hours long. Math has left the building.

03:30 — The credits roll

Imen looks at the dark sky, emotionally destroyed by a fictional wedding, and whispers: "totally worth it."

Next morning — The classroom

25 children, full energy, zero mercy. Imen runs on tea, orange-blossom coffee, and the memory of last night's plot twist.
